The rock band, RUSH

RUSH is a three-membered progressive rock band from Toronto, ON, Canada. They consist of singer and bassist, Geddy Lee, guitarist, Alex Lifeson, and drummer Neil Peart. The band has been in existence for over 40 years. Their music is technically as sharp as it comes and their sound takes you to a different galaxy altogether.

Another site completely devoted to RUSH is here. I find this to be a well designed and dynamic website. Many aspects of the HTML source that I am reading, I can understand, like some of the unordered lists, the headers, the various headline tags, the image insertions, etc. However, there are also many aspects of this code that I do not understand, especially the script directly underneath the body tag. It appears to have something to do with Google Analytics, but I don't have such a great sense about what it is meant to do. There is another section of code near the bottom that has some interfacing with Facebook, but I do not know precisely what it is meant to do. There are also many HTML tag types that I do not yet have familiarity for, such as the article, span, and noscript tags. So, on the whole, for this really nice looking and dynamic website, there are certain parts that I am beginning to understand, while there are other locations that I still have considerable confusions about.

The Los Angeles Lakers

I have been a Laker fan since 1981 as I was raised in Los Angeles. I have witnessed three different championship dynasties in my lifetime:

  1. The Showtime Years (1980-1987). Notable players include Earvin Magic Johnson, Kareem Abdul Jabbar, James Worthy, and Michael Cooper. Championships include 1980, 1982, 1985, and 1987.
  2. The Shaq-Kobe Years (1996-2004). Notable players include Shaquille ONeal, Kobe Bryant, Derek Fisher, Robert Horry, and Rick Fox. Championships include 2000, 2001, and 2002.
  3. The Kobe-Pau Years (2008-2011). Notable players include Kobe Bryant, Pau Gasol, Derek Fisher, Lamar Odom, and Andrew Bynum. Championships include 2009 and 2010.

Personality Typology Theories

I am very interested in understanding what makes people tick. Likewise, I am fascinated when certain personality type models can predict and somewhat approximate how I perceive the world, and how respond to all sorts of stimuli. Below are two such typological models that I enjoy learning and applying...

Myers-Briggs

Sixteen types. Cognitively-oriented model based on how we perceive information and make judgements

Enneagram

Nine types. All based on various ego fixations. Circular model.

Enneagram Type (number) Descriptive Name Passion/Fixation
One The Reformer Resentment/Judging
Two The Helper Pride/Flattery
Three The Achiever Deceit/Vanity
Four The Individualist Envy/Melancholy
Five The Investigator Avarice/Stinginess
Six The Loyalist Fear/Cowardice
Seven The Enthusiast Gluttony/Planning
Eight The Challenger Lust/Vengeance
Nine The Peacemaker Sloth/Indolence
